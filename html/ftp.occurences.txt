chapters/www_genomesCbriggsae/xml/genomesCbriggsae.xml:<para>A hybrid approach combining whole-genome assembly with whole-genome shotgun sequencing (WGS) was adopted to sequence the <emphasis>C. briggsae</emphasis> genome (<link linkend="bib46">Stein et al., 2003</link><!--; PMID 14624247-->). The <emphasis>C. briggsae</emphasis> strain <ulink url="http://www.wormbase.org/db/get?name=AF16;class=Strain" role="_blank">AF16</ulink> was targeted for physical map construction (<link linkend="bib33">Marra et al., 1997</link><!--; PMID 9371743-->) and whole genome sequencing. A total number of 2.068 million reads, representing an approximately 10-fold coverage of the entire genome, were assembled into 5,341 contigs and linked together into 889 gapped supercontigs containing 105.6 Mbp of DNA sequence. This assembly (named cb25.agp8) consists of 463 sequence superconfigs placed into 142 FPCs and covers 102,431,873 bp and is publicly available at the <ulink url="ftp://ftp.wormbase.org/pub/wormbase/genomes/briggsae/genome_assembly/cb25.agp8" role="_blank">WormBase FTP site</ulink>. This assembly is estimated to cover 98% of the entire <emphasis>C. briggsae</emphasis> genome with a sequencing accuracy of approximately 99.98%. Therefore the <emphasis>C. briggsae</emphasis> genome size is approximately 104 Mbp, a size similar to but slightly larger than that of the <emphasis>C. elegans</emphasis> genome (102 Mbp, according to WormBase WS160). Recently, the construction of a genetic map using single nucleotide polymorphic (SNP) markers has positioned 100 Mb along the six chromosomes and refined the sequence map (<link linkend="bib21">Hillier et al., 2005</link><!--; PMID 16339362-->).</para>
